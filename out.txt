12A B S T R A C T
Customer churn is a critical challenge  for telecom companies, as losing subscribers directly
impacts revenue and growth. This study investigates the demographic, financial, and
service-related factors that influence churn behavior. To begin, an exploratory analysis was
carried out using bar charts, pie charts, histograms, and box plots to visualize customer
characteristics and churn patterns.  These plots provided initial insights into variable
distributions and highlighted potential associations worth testing.
To formally assess these relationships, categorical variables were analyzed using the Chi-
squared test, while the Shapiro–W ilk test was applied to numerical variables to check
normality and determine whether parametric or non-parametric tests were appropriate.
The results show that demographic factors such as SeniorCitizen and Dependents, as well
as service features including InternetService, OnlineSecurity, TechSupport, StreamingT V,
and StreamingMovies, have a significant association with churn. In contrast, PhoneService
does not show a meaningful impact. Company policy-related features such as Contract and
PaymentMethod also strongly influen ce customer retention. Normality tests revealed that
numerical variables do not follow a normal distribution at the 5% significance level,
requiring non-parametric approaches. Hypothesis testing further indica ted that there is
insufficient statistical evidence to conclude that churned customers differ from retained
customers in terms of tenure, MonthlyCharges, or T otalCharges.
Overall, the analysis highlights that churn is driven more by service quality, contract type,
and customer demographics than by cost alone. These findings sugges t that improving
support services, offering flexible contracts, and tailoring engageme nt strategies for
vulnerable customer groups can help reduce churn and strengthen customer loyalty.Table of Contents4INTRODUCTION

3I N T R O D U C T I O N
Background of the study
This data set contains customer details of telecommunication company called “SpeedyCall” which is
in USA. In this analysis we are interested to know whether a customer will churn or not according to
customer behavior.
objectives
To examine the impact of demographic variables on customer churn and use visualizations to
analyze churn patterns across other key variables .
To evaluate the impact of financial factors on customer churn.
To analyze how service-related features influence customer churn (eg - PhoneService,
InternetService, OnlineSecurity, T echSupport).
To analyze how value-added service features influence customer churn (eg - StreamingT V,
StreamingMovies).
To evaluate how organizational policy-based variables impact customer satisfaction (eg -
Contract, PaymentMethod, MonthlyCharges), which are some of the key variables related to
company policies.
significance of the study
Understanding customer churn is crucial for telecommunication companies because acquiring new
customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones. A churn analysis helps the
company identify patterns and key factors such as service quality, pricing, contract type, or
customer support, that lead customers to discontinue their services. By studying churn,  
telecommunication companies can predict customer behavior, implement targeted retention
strategies, improve customer satisfaction, and optimize business policies. Ultimately, churn analysis
enables the company to reduce revenue loss, maintain a stable customer base, and gain a
competitive advantage in a highly saturated market.

Demographic variables such as SeniorCitizen and Dependents show a significant association
with customer churn, indicating that age-related status and family responsibilities may
influence churn behavior.
Financial factors like Monthly Charges and T otal Charges showed no significant difference
between churned and retained customers, suggesting cost alone may not drive churn.
Service features like InternetService, OnlineSecurity, and T echSupport show a strong link to
churn, unlike PhoneService, which has no significant impact, possibly because most customers
have phone service by default, making it less influential in churn decisions.
StreamingT V and StreamingMovies show a significant association with churn, suggesting that
even small differences in how customers use these value-added services can influence whether
they stay or leave.
Customers’ decisions to stay or leave seem to depend not just on how much they pay, but also
on how they pay and the type of contract they have. Since Contract and PaymentMethod show
a strong link to churn, it’ s clear that company rules around billing and plans can play a big role
in keeping customers or losing them.
10To reduce customer churn, the company should focus on improving services and making contracts
more flexible. Since one out of four customers has already left, it’ s important to give extra support
to groups like senior citizens and families with dependents, and to strengthen services such as
OnlineSecurity, T echSupport, and streaming options. Clear and flexible contracts, along with easier
payment methods, can also help customers feel more satisfied and stay longer.
